tea is one of the mainstays of civilization

  1. Use Indian or Ceylon tea
  2. Use a teapot
  3. Warm the pot over direct heat
  4. Six spoons of leaves per 1 litre
  5. Let the leaves move around the pot - no bags or strainers
  6. Take the pot to the boiling kettle
  7. Stir or shake the pot
  8. Drink out of a tall, mug-shaped tea cup
  9. Don't add creamy milk
  10. Add milk to the tea, not vice versa
  11. Don't add sugar

If you still have trouble brewing a tea, please follow the guidelines set out by the International Organization for Standardization:

The method consists in extracting of soluble substances in dried tea leaf, contained in a porcelain or earthenware pot, by means of freshly boiling water, pouring of the liquor into a white porcelain or earthenware bowl, examination of the organoleptic properties of the infused leaf, and of the liquor with or without milk, or both.
